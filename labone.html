ECE 3400 Lab Work

Fall 2019 - Group #23

Lab 1 - Microcontroller

Our Github for Lab 1

The objective of this lab was to test an Arduino Uno board, as well as eventually create a moving robot by the end of the lab.
We inserted a 300 Ohm Resistor in series for safety to avoid damaging any of our components, and tested all the pins.
For Lab 1, we first tested an LED as well as, in the process, the connectors of our Arduino Uno microcontroller.
After testing all the pins to see if our board was working, we attempted to change the brightness of the LED as instructed in the lab.
We encountered some trouble in getting an output for the brightness, as well as some issues with the connections in our circuit.
Meanwhile, we also started working on connecting the parts of our robot and circuit, mainly the servo.
We got into some issues getting our servo to work correctly, until realized that we were supposed to connect it to the Arduino
rather than the battery; it was acting erraticly before we fixed this oversight.
Eventually, we better organized our circuit to fit on our robot and managed to get the breadboard on top of it.
Our wire management made our work and testing much easier, compared to the usual mess of wires that a circuit usually becomes.

After all the hardware handiwork, we returned to coding to program our board and Parallax servo to move in a controlled manner;
we tested front and back movement as well as making the robot turn. While working, we noticed that the robot would do things completely
unexpectedly, such as being able to move with our code being full of errors, so much so that the robot shouldn't have been able to do anything.
Once we understood and figured out how to control our robot through the software, we began to test different ways we wanted
the robot to move, such as tweaking the speed of the wheels so we could eventually make it move nearly straight.
This part of the lab took the most trial and error, because, as we quickly learned, we had to make our software work for the hardware (eg. the wheels),
rather than the other way around. We predict that this will probably be a common theme in our future ECE 3400 labs and projects.

Finally, we got our robot to move in a specific pattern: we made it move in a square, or at least very close to that.
We got it to move straight in one direction for some time, and programmed it to turn 90 degrees after that time.
